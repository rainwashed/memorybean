An antecedent of something, such as an event or organization, has happened or existed before it and can be similar to it.

When you beget something, you cause it to happen or create it.

Concentric circles, gradually increasing in size, spread out from a common central point.

When something ensues, it happens after or as a result of another event.

Gestation is the process by which a new idea, plan, or piece of work develops in your mind.

An inception of something is its beginning or start.

An incipient situation or quality is just beginning to appear or develop.

The lexicon of a particular subject or language is all the words, phrases, and terms associated with it.

Something that is nascent is just starting to develop and is expected to become stronger and bigger in time.

You are applying parlance when you use words or expressions that are used by a particular group of people who have a unique way of speaking; for example, “descendants” is often used in place of “children” in legal parlance.

A first event is a precursor to a second event if the first event is responsible for the development or existence of the second.

A predecessor comes before someone else in a job or is an ancestor of someone.

Progeny are children or descendants.

A seminal article, book, piece of music or other work has many new ideas; additionally, it has great influence on generating ideas for future work.

Verbiage is an excessive use of words to convey something that could be expressed using fewer words; it can also be the manner or style in which someone uses words.

A vernacular is the ordinary and everyday language spoken by people in a particular country or region that differs from the literary or standard written language of that area.

derivative

dih-RIV-uh-tiv

Context

There are thousands of words in English that are derivatives of Latin, that is, they use Latin words in their formation. Some examples of derivatives or words borrowed from the Latin root ven include “convene,” “venue,” and “invent.” Most new English words are derivatives from either Latin or Greek words; English uses those classical languages because they are unchanging and it’s always been done that way.

Definition
  1. A derivative is something borrowed from something else, such as an English word that comes from another language.
